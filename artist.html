ABOUT
Members of Prabhakar Pachpute's family have worked in the coal mines of Chandrapur, Central India, for three generations. He frequently draws directly onto walls and has created spectacular mural installations and immersive environments of wondrous landscapes and dreamlike figures. These figures are workers with their faces sometimes replaced by incorporating actual light switches, reflecting both his surrealistic aesthetic and the subsuming of the individual into the larger labour force.

ABOUT
There are many characters conjured up by Nikhil Chopra, one of India’s leading performance artists and the current artist-in-residence at the Metropolitan Museum of Art. Whether roving the Met or walking from Athens to Kassel for Documenta 14 in 2017, contemplative drawing is integral to Chopra’s practice. Chopra’s other whimsical characters have appeared at the Second Yinchuan Biennale in China in 2018, the Sharjah Biennial 12 in 2015, and the 53rd Venice Biennale in 2009.
